In general, we denote by G an arbitrary graph with n vertices, m edges, and k components. Using the above procedure for each component of G, we obtain as a result of the graph, called a spanning forest. The number of remote edges in this procedure is called cyclic rank (or cyclomatic number) of G and is denoted by γ (G). It is obvious that γ (G) = m - n + k is a nonnegative integer.
